# Behavioural Question Situations (STAR Format)

---

## 1. Pre-Bids (Tilt)

**Situation**  
While analysing Tilt’s purchase funnel, I saw a 35% drop-off between bidding and purchase, suggesting something in the experience was preventing users from completing transactions.

**Task**  
Identify the root cause and improve conversion.

**Action**  
- Analysed funnel data and session replays.  
- Found pre-bids converted at only ~1.1% vs 62% for live bids.  
- Discovered most failed pre-bids were on items that never went to auction.  
- Hypothesised that pre-bids were diverting users away from live auctions, the core experience.  
- Ran a quick experiment removing pre-biddable items from the home feed instead of removing the full feature.

**Result**  
- 15% increase in users entering live rooms.  
- Directional improvement in bidding and purchase.  

**Reflection**  
Simplifying the user experience can produce better results by going back to first principles.

## 2. Building Automated Risk Decisioning (Stenn)

**Situation**  
Credit risk and compliance decisions were manual, inconsistent, and slow.

**Task**  
Create a scalable system for automated decision-making with oversight.

**Action**  
- Facilitated cross-functional workshops.  
- Mapped decision-making and surfaced conflicting heuristics.  
- Created a shared decision framework.  
- Scoped automation to reliable regions/cases.  
- Delegated delivery and maintained governance.

**Result**  
- Automated risk decisions increased from <10% to >80%.  
- Compliance decisions largely automated.  
- Faster processing and improved consistency.  

**Reflection**  
Anti-fraud required a semi-automated approach due to inconsistent deal inputs.

## 3. Building Trust & Safety From Scratch (Tilt)
